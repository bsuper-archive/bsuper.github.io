Great Podcasts I Recommend

12 Jun 2016 on Other

This summer, I spend at least 40 minutes in my car and about 2 hours on a bus to work each workday. That's a lot of time to kill! What I can't stand are the songs they play on the radio - 94.9, 97.3, 99.7, 101.3, 101.7. They are repetitive and trashy, maybe except Taylor Swift, whom I usually enjoy listening to. As I spend more time on the road, I have realized that podcasts are the way to go for long commutes. Depending on your selection, they can be interesting, educational, and make you feel like you've learned something useful during the day. Here are some my recommendations, which I'll continually update over time as I discover more:

  1. Planet Money

    I started listening to Planet Money in high school. The podcast focuses on local and global topics, such as like hiding a million dollars in plain sight, countries selling citizenship, and ticket scalping. They even setup an offshore company to understand how it works. Each episode is an investigation into the topic, with a several interviews with experts and ordinary people and is well narrated. I've learned a lot from just listening to these podcasts. Here are some of my recommendations:

    • What happens when a country, Argentina, refuses to pay its debts?
    • How the government setup a fake bank to launder drug money and uncover the drug cartel?
    • How does a scam work and who gets scammed?
    • Getting caught insider trading.

  2. Startup

    Ever wanted to know what is it really like to have a startup? Well, you can finally do so, without the spending the long hours and many $$ needed to actually start one. In Startup, the hosts follow startups through several important stages in their lives. Each episode involves recorded conversations between founders, partners, and even family members, who often feel a big impact when one of them is running a startup. In the first season, the show follows Gimlet Media, the company behind this show, and shows the process of starting a startup, including getting investors, communicating with the spouse, and finding a name for the startup. The second season follows a online dating site Dating Ring and how the startup life is like for female founders. In the current season, the show tells the stories of several startups, including Justin.tv, the predecessor to Twitch, Pirate Joe, a company that smuggles Trader Joe goods across to the Canadian border, and a food delivery company called Bento, which is undergoing a possible shutdown. It's a fascinating insight into the world of startups, and I would highly recommend it if you ever wanted to know about startups or wanted to start one yourself.

  3. Reply All
  4. Talking Machines

    This is a new podcast that I recently discovered and focuses on the theory and applications of machine learning. It appears to be both academic and practical and I'd love to check this out more.

  5. Partially Derivative

    I've also discovered this one recently. This one focuses on the world of data science.

  6. Serial

    I'm looking forward to listening to this podcast. Each season tells a captivating story from the beginning to the end. I'll let you guys know what I think soon!
